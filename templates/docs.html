IX — Maps & Levels

And that’s the main motivation for starting this blog—to keep myself up to date on new technologies that interest me and document my tinkerings. The idea 1 is that using the blog format to report on my experiments will help me focus and also make me more diligent in researching my topics. After all, the Ctrl+C goal is to write about things 2 that are new to me, so trying to keep the amount of misinformation I’m unwillingly (but inevitably) going to spread to a minimum is probably a good idea, as I’m sure the experts will be far too eager to chime in and correct me

2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9 10

  • (Mostly) keyboard-driven modal user interface (similar to Vim and Blender)
  • Create maps having as many as 999 levels of varying dimensions of up to 6,666 × 6,666 cells per level
  • Handy map manipulation tools (cut/copy/paste, nudge, resize, move/fill/erase area, and many others)
  • Linked teleports, stairways and doors for easy navigation
  • Various cell annotation options (e.g. numbers, icons, comments)
  • Unlimited undo & redo history
  • Extensive theming support & many awesome themes included
  • Text labels
